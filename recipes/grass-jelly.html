Grass Jelly Drink

Description

Grass jelly drink is a refreshing and unique beverage originating from various Asian cuisines. This delightful drink features cubes of grass jelly, which is made from the leaves of the Mesona chinensis plant. The jelly has a slightly herbal and earthy flavor, offering a distinctive taste experience.

Served over ice, Grass Jelly Drink provides a refreshing combination of textures and flavors. The tender and chewy grass jelly cubes are immersed in a lightly sweetened syrup, typically made with sugar or honey. This drink is often enhanced with additional elements like fresh fruit, coconut milk, or evaporated milk, adding further layers of taste and creaminess. Known for its cooling properties and one-of-a-kind texture, Grass Jelly Drink is a popular choice, particularly during hot summer months, as it delivers a satisfying blend of sweetness, herbal undertones, and a pleasantly slippery sensation.

Ingredients

  • 2 Packages of Grass Jelly Powder
  • 20 Grams of Cornstarch
  • 7 Tablespoons Sugar
  • 1100 Milliliters of Water
  • 2 Black Tea Bags
  • 350 Milliliters of Milk

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all the ingredients and add approximately 200ml of water. Mix well until the powder is fully dissolved.
  2. In a separate saucepan, bring the remaining 800ml of water to a boil.
  3. Slowly pour the mixture from the bowl into the boiling water while stirring continuously.
  4. Continue heating the mixture for 1-2 minutes, stirring occasionally, until large bubbles form on the surface.
  5. Remove the saucepan from the heat and transfer the grass jelly mixture to a separate container.
  6. Allow the mixture to cool down for several hours in the refrigerator until it sets into a jelly-like consistency.
  7. In a saucepan, heat around 100ml of water.
  8. Add 2 bags of black tea and 3 tablespoons of sugar to the heated water.
  9. Simmer the tea and sugar mixture for 2-3 minutes.
  10. Turn off the heat and let the tea steep for a few minutes.
  11. Add approximately 300ml to 350ml of milk to the saucepan and stir to combine.
  12. Cover the pot and refrigerate the mixture for several hours to allow the flavors to blend and the tea to chill.
  13. Remove the tea bags from the mixture.
  14. Cut the set grass jelly into cubes and carefully transfer them into serving containers.
  15. Pour the chilled milk tea over the grass jelly cubes.
  16. Serve and enjoy your refreshing grass jelly milk tea.
