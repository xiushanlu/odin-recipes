Matcha Mochi Brownies

Description

Matcha Mochi Brownies are a unique and delightful dessert that combines the delicate flavors of matcha green tea with the chewy texture of mochi. These green tea-infused treats offer a taste experience that is both subtly sweet and pleasantly earthy. The brownies are made with matcha powder, which gives them their vibrant green color and imparts a distinctively refreshing flavor.

With every bite, you'll be greeted by the soft and chewy texture of the mochi layer, adding a delightful contrast to the tender crumb of the brownies. The matcha flavor shines through, providing a gentle bitterness that is balanced by the natural sweetness of the dessert. Matcha Mochi Brownies are a unique twist on traditional brownies, offering a delightful combination of Japanese flavors and an enjoyable chewy texture. They are a delightful treat for matcha enthusiasts and anyone looking to explore new and exciting dessert options.

Ingredients

  • 1 Cup Glutinous Rice Flour
  • 1/2 Cup Granlated Sugar
  • 1 Teaspoon Baking Powder
  • 2 Tablespoons Vegetable Oil
  • 1 Large Egg
  • 1 Cup Whole Milk
  • 1 Tablespoon High-Quality Matcha
  • A Pinch of Sea Salt

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  2. Line an 8" x 8" square baking pan with parchment paper.
  3. In a large bowl, combine glutinous rice flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Then sift in the matcha powder. Whisk all the dry ingredients together until well combined.
  4. In another bowl, whisk together the egg, oil, and milk.
  5. Pour the wet ingredients over the dry ingredients in the large bowl.
  6. Use a spatula to mix everything together until there are no lumps and the mixture is well combined.
  7. Pour the batter into the prepared pan.
  8. Gently tap the pan a few times to remove any air bubbles.
  9. Place the pan in the center of the oven and bake at 350°F for 35-40 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the brownies comes out clean.
  10. Once baked, remove the pan from the oven and let the brownies cool on a wire rack.
  11. Slice the cooled brownies into 16 small squares and dust them with additional matcha powder.
